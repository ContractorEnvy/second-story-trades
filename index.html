Our Story

A second story is something you build.

You don't tear down the first floor to get it — you build on top of what's already standing. That's what we do here, twice over.

We take churches that are falling apart, and homes where a senior, a veteran, or a person with a disability can't safely use their own bathroom or kitchen — buildings with history and heart but not enough hands or money to fix them — and we give them a second story. Restored. Safe. Standing tall again.

And we do the exact same thing for the people who do the work. Most are coming home, coming back, starting over — people with a first story they can't undo. We don't ask them to erase it. We help them build the next one on top of it: paid, on the job, learning real trades from real tradespeople.

They trade a past for a craft. A shot for a skill. When the job's done, the building has a new chapter — and so do they.

Two tracks. Every barrier removed.

All pay comes as a weekly stipend that includes transportation to and from the job site — so not having a vehicle never keeps anyone out.

Track One

Skilled Labor

$650–$750 / week
  • 3–5 trainees per cohort
  • Hands-on across demo, plumbing, tile, finish
  • Taught by working tradespeople
  • Trade skills + a real job placement

Track Two

Project Management

$800–$1,000 / week
  • 1–2 trainees per cohort
  • Runs the job under Coach Kev
  • Learns crews, budgets & scheduling
  • On the path to a GC license via Contractor Envy
Try before you commit — to a whole career. On a single project, apprentices rotate through several trades taught by several contractors. They discover what they're good at and what they love, then we focus their path — and set up the hire.
